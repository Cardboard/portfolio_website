I'm writing to you by the light of five mushrooms.

I'm out here somewhere if you'd care to look —

if you'd dare take a look.

I'm out here in wild nature. -

Watch your steps,

where they fall,

as you make your way through foliage I've trampled.

Long ago I made my way,

by moonlight and sunlight,

through the forest,

the trees,

through life.

I'm embedded in the vines now, if you can believe that.

-

We all are.

The green you exude in life grows

forests in other parts of the world.

How neat is that!

Firmly locate your feet —

in dampened dusk boots

— in the moist recesses of

abundant nature,

until you find me.
